**Prefer data-aware scheduling over time-aware scheduling.** When the real trigger is "new data exists," schedule on the data, not the clock. In Airflow 3 the producer declares `outlets=[Asset(...)]` and the consumer sets `schedule=[Asset(...)]`; on Airflow 2.x the equivalent is Datasets (`outlets`/`schedule` on `Dataset`). Event-driven asset watchers are an Airflow 3.x feature only and still resolve within scheduler poll latency, so treat cross-pipeline freshness as low-latency-eventual, not instantaneous. Reserve cron schedules for genuinely time-driven work (e.g., a daily report due at a fixed hour regardless of upstream). Data-aware scheduling reduces wasted runs on absent data; it does not by itself eliminate any failure mode.
