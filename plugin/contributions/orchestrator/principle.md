**Orchestrate, don't compute.** Airflow's job in a data pipeline is to coordinate work, validate it, and emit lineage — not to crunch data in-process. Keep heavy or large-scale transformation in the engine built for it (dbt, Spark, the warehouse) and let the DAG schedule, sequence, check, and record. A task that pulls a large dataset into the worker to transform it is a design smell; push that compute down and have the DAG orchestrate and verify the result. Named engines here are examples, not mandates.
