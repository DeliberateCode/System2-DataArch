**Govern the interface, not just the data.** Reliable pipelines come from governing how data is exposed and consumed, resting on three pillars: (1) a canonical/semantic layer so consumers share definitions instead of re-deriving them; (2) a small set of single-source-of-truth datasets rather than many divergent forks of the same entity; and (3) provenance on every delivered artifact so any output can be traced to its sources, queries, and versions. Before producing a new dataset, ask whether an existing canonical entity should be reused or extended instead of forked.
